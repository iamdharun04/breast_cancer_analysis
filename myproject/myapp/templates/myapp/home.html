Introduction

Breast cancer is a complex disease with many factors influencing its development and prognosis. Our project aims to utilize advanced statistical methods to analyze breast cancer data, including linear regression, clustering, and principal component analysis (PCA).

Linear Regression

Linear regression is a powerful tool for understanding the relationship between variables in breast cancer datasets. By fitting linear models to the data, we can identify significant predictors and assess their impact on cancer outcomes.

Clustering

Clustering techniques allow us to group similar breast cancer cases together based on a variety of features. This enables us to identify subtypes of breast cancer and explore potential differences in treatment response and prognosis.

Principal Component Analysis (PCA)

PCA is a dimensionality reduction technique that helps us visualize high-dimensional breast cancer data in a lower-dimensional space. By identifying patterns and relationships between variables, PCA aids in uncovering underlying structures within the data.
